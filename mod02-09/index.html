A traditionally a red, red-brown, or "mahogany" or "maroon" colored non-Dutched Cocoa, layer cake, layered with white cream cheese icing or Ermine frosting (sometimes called "cooked roux" frosting). It has a distinctive fluffy texture owing to the gas-producing reaction of alkaline buttermilk with acidic vinegar and/or acidic non-alkalized cocoa.The cake is commonly served on Christmas or Valentine's Day. Common modern red velvet cake is made with red dye, however the red color was originally due to non-Dutched anthocyanin-rich cocoa. Common ingredients include: buttermilk, butter, cocoa, vinegar, and flour for the cake; beetroot or red food coloring for the color.
